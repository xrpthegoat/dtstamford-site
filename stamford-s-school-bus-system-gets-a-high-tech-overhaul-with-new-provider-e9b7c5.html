Stamford’s School Bus System Gets a High-Tech Overhaul with New Provider

Stamford’s school buses are getting a high-tech upgrade with Zum, bringing real-time tracking and air-conditioned rides starting 2027.

Uploaded · July 21, 2026 at 7:44 AM ET

Stamford residents, especially those with school-aged children, should take note: the city’s public school transportation system is undergoing a major transformation. Starting in the 2027–2028 school year, the Stamford Board of Education will switch from its longtime provider, First Student, to a Silicon Valley-based company called Zum, which will bring 160 new air-conditioned buses and a tech-driven service model to the district [1][2]. This change, approved unanimously on June 23, 2026, marks a significant shift in how students commute and could influence neighborhood dynamics, transit access, and housing demand across the city.

What's happening

The Stamford Board of Education voted to award Zum Services Inc. a five-year, $147 million contract to manage the district’s school bus operations beginning with summer school in 2027 [1][2]. Zum, headquartered in Redwood City, California, currently serves school districts in Los Angeles, Boston, and Omaha, among others [1]. The new contract will replace the existing fleet with 160 brand new, air-conditioned buses, a move intended to improve student comfort, especially during hot summer months [1][2].

Zum’s system includes a digital platform called the Connected Mobility Experience (CMX), which will allow parents to track their child’s bus in real time via a map-based interface, receive notifications when a driver is approaching a stop, and verify rider status [2]. The platform also enables parents to cancel rides remotely if a student is absent or taking an alternate route [2]. Drivers will receive AI-powered routing, student-specific health alerts (such as EpiPen use or nonverbal communication needs), and real-time performance analytics [1][2]. Zum claims a 98% on-time performance average across its current operations and reports a zero driver shortage, attributing this to competitive pay, benefits, and modern facilities [1][2].

The new transportation model will require a dedicated maintenance and operations hub. A proposed bus depot on Courtland Avenue has drawn attention and some opposition from East Side residents, though the final site has not been confirmed in public documents [3]. The Zoning Board has reviewed related applications, but the exact location and design of the depot remain under review [4]. The full rollout is scheduled to begin in the 2027–2028 academic year, with the transition expected to be phased in over the summer [2].

The broader context: modernizing school transportation

This shift reflects a growing national trend toward outsourcing school transportation to tech-enabled providers. Zum’s model emphasizes data-driven routing, real-time tracking, and driver retention through improved working conditions—features increasingly expected by families in urban and suburban districts alike [2]. The move also signals a broader investment in infrastructure and reliability, which can affect how families perceive the quality of public education in Stamford.

While the sources do not specify whether the new depot will impact traffic, noise, or property values in surrounding areas, the fact that it is being planned in a residential zone raises questions about long-term neighborhood impacts. The city’s Zoning Board has already begun reviewing related applications, suggesting that public input will play a role in the final decision [4]. However, the exact size, design, and environmental review details for the depot were not disclosed in the available sources.
