Stamford Homes with Top-Rated Schools

Stamford homes with top-rated schools are in higher demand — and shorter supply — than almost any other segment of the local market. Knowing the school boundaries before you fall in love with a house is the single most important step for families.

How Stamford's school district works

Stamford Public Schools is a single unified district with 20+ schools. Your assigned school is determined by your home's address, not your neighborhood name. School boundaries can run down a single street — two houses apart can be zoned for different elementaries. Always verify the exact zoning with SPS before making an offer.

Neighborhoods families consistently choose for schools

  • North Stamford — zoned for Westover Magnet and Rogers International; larger lots, quieter streets
  • Shippan — waterfront area with a tight community feel; families return for the elementary school culture
  • Turn of River — suburban feel with access to well-regarded middle school feeder patterns
  • Westover — close to Westover Magnet Elementary, one of Stamford's highest-rated schools

Magnet school programs worth knowing

Stamford runs several citywide magnet programs open to any resident regardless of address: Rogers International (IB curriculum K–12), Westover Magnet Elementary (STEM focus), and Dolan School (arts integration). Admission is by lottery — apply early, and don't let a school boundary be the only reason you rule out a house.

What to ask before you make an offer

  • Confirm the exact elementary, middle, and high school assignment for that parcel
  • Ask whether the address qualifies for magnet school applications
  • Check whether any boundary rezoning is pending (SPS reviews boundaries periodically)
