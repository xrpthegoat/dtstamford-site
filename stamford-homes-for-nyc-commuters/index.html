Stamford Homes for NYC Commuters

Stamford homes for NYC commuters offer something Manhattan and Brooklyn can't: real space, a yard, lower property taxes, and a 45–60 minute Metro-North express back to Grand Central. It's why Stamford has been Connecticut's fastest-growing city for a decade.

The commute — what it actually looks like

The Stamford Metro-North station runs express trains to Grand Central Terminal in as little as 45 minutes. The New Haven Line is one of the most frequent on the Northeast Corridor — trains run every 20–30 minutes during peak hours. If you live within 10 minutes of the station, your door-to-desk time often beats a Brooklyn-to-Midtown subway commute.

What your money buys compared to the city

At Stamford's median price of roughly $600,000 you get a 3-bedroom single-family home with a driveway and yard. The same budget in Park Slope or Astoria buys a 1-bedroom condo. State income tax in Connecticut is lower than New York City's combined state-and-city rate for most income levels, and there's no NYC resident surcharge.

Best neighborhoods for NYC commuters

  • Downtown Stamford — walk to the station; condos and new construction from the high $300s
  • Harbor Point — waterfront building living, 7-minute walk to Metro-North
  • Shippan — single-family homes near Long Island Sound, 10-minute drive to the station
  • Glenbrook — entry-level houses, close to the Glenbrook station on the local line
  • North Stamford — larger lots for buyers who want space and don't mind the drive to the station

What to watch for as a buyer

Stamford's commuter market moves fast. Homes near the station that are priced right routinely receive multiple offers. Work with a local agent who can alert you the day a listing hits and help you structure a competitive offer without overpaying.
