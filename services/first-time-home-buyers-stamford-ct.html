The step-by-step, so you know what to expect

1. Budget first. Before looking at a single listing, we work out what you're comfortable paying monthly — not just what a lender will approve. That includes property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees on condos. See what income you need to buy in Stamford for context.

2. Pre-approval. A pre-approval letter is what makes your offer real — in Stamford, listing agents expect one attached. I'll refer you to local lenders who answer their phones and know first-time buyer programs. This step is free and doesn't lock you into anything.

3. Down-payment help (CHFA). You likely need less cash than you think — common loan programs allow roughly 3–3.5% down for qualifying buyers. On top of that, the Connecticut Housing Finance Authority (CHFA) offers eligible first-time buyers below-market-rate first mortgages, the Down Payment Assistance Program (DAP) second loan for down payment and closing costs, and the Time To Own forgivable loan — all subject to income and sales-price limits, homebuyer-education requirements on some programs, and available funding. Terms change, so we confirm current details at chfa.org and with a participating lender. More background: how much you need for a down payment and first-time buyer programs in Stamford.

4. The search. We define your must-haves and shop the real market — starter condos downtown and in Glenbrook and Springdale, capes and colonials further out. I keep a running page of Stamford homes suited to first-time buyers, and you can browse everything live at the search page.

5. Offer, inspection, attorney. When you find the one, I pull comparable sales, recommend a number, and write the offer. After acceptance: inspection (you can negotiate or walk based on findings), then your attorney — Connecticut closings run through real estate attorneys, and I'll help you line one up. Budget for closing costs here too.

6. Clear to close. Appraisal, mortgage commitment, final walkthrough, keys. Most purchases run roughly 45–60 days from accepted offer to closing — here's the full timeline.

FAQ

How much down payment do I need as a first-time buyer?

Usually less than people think — 20% is not required. Common programs allow roughly 3–3.5% down for qualifying buyers, and CHFA down-payment assistance can help eligible buyers cover part of the rest. A lender confirms your exact number; I'll connect you with one.

What is CHFA?

The Connecticut Housing Finance Authority — the state's housing finance agency. For eligible buyers it offers below-market-rate mortgages, the DAP second loan, and the Time To Own forgivable loan. Limits and funding change, so we verify current terms at chfa.org and with a participating lender.

What does working with you cost?

Buyer-agent compensation is negotiable and agreed in writing before we tour homes. In many transactions the seller's side offers to cover some or all of it, but that's confirmed deal by deal — no surprises at closing. Full breakdown: what a real estate agent costs in Stamford.
