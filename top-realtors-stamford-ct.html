The best real estate agent in Stamford depends entirely on your deal. An $8M estate sale, a first condo, a 1031 exchange, and a downtown rental are four different jobs — and Stamford has genuinely excellent people for each of them. Below, the agents and teams with the strongest public review signals as of July 2026, organized by specialty rather than ranked.

What makes a great Stamford agent

Stamford is an unusually wide market: the team price ranges on this page alone run from $85K to $8.8M. A great agent here knows their lane inside that range — the buildings, the streets, the price behavior of their specific segment — and can prove it with recent closed sales, not slogans. The other tell is a public review trail: dozens or hundreds of named, dated reviews are hard to fake and easy for you to read. Every agent below clears both bars.

Methodology Built from public review profiles — Zillow's Stamford agent directory and each team's own public website — plus specialty coverage. All figures are as of July 2026 and will drift over time; verify current numbers on each linked profile. No agent paid to be included, and this is not a ranking: agents are grouped by specialty, in no particular order within groups.

How to choose from this list

The short version: match the specialty to your deal, then let recent reviews and recent sales in your segment break the tie. A 5.0 average built on estate sales tells you little about how a team handles a $300K condo — and vice versa.
  • Match the lane. A condo purchase, an estate sale, and an investment property are different jobs. Start with the specialty group above that fits yours.
  • Read the newest reviews in your price range, not the overall star average. Named, dated reviews from deals like yours are the strongest signal on this page.
  • Ask about the last 12 months in your segment. "How many condos like mine did you close this year?" is a fair question, and good agents answer it happily.
  • Interview two or three. Responsiveness, straight answers, and how they handle a hard question are the real product — you'll feel the difference in one call.
  • Verify the license. Every CT agent's license status is public via the state's eLicense lookup.
