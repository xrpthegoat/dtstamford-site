Living in North Stamford

North Stamford is the city's largest and most rural section: winding roads, wooded acreage, and larger single-family homes set well back from the street. It borders open space including the Bartlett Arboretum & Gardens and Mianus River Park, giving it a private, tree-lined character distinct from the rest of the city.

Getting around & commuting

North Stamford is oriented toward drivers, with easy access to the Merritt Parkway and a straightforward route down to Downtown Stamford and its Metro-North station for trains toward Grand Central.

North Stamford: quick answers

Is North Stamford more rural?

Yes — North Stamford is the city's largest and most rural section, with wooded lots, winding roads, and larger homes near open space like the Bartlett Arboretum.

How much are homes in North Stamford?

Homes are typically larger single-family properties on sizable lots, with many sales at $1M and above (estimated) depending on acreage and finish.

Does North Stamford have a train station?

North Stamford is car-oriented with Merritt Parkway access; the nearest Metro-North service is at the Stamford Transportation Center a short drive south.
