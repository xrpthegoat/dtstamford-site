Moving from NYC to Stamford, CT

More space, a real backyard or a waterfront condo, and Grand Central still ~50 minutes away. Here's the honest rundown on trading Manhattan for Stamford.

Last updated · July 2026

Moving from NYC to Stamford puts Grand Central about 50 minutes away while giving you roughly twice the home for your housing dollar. Many movers also drop New York City's local income tax in the process. Here's the honest breakdown: commute times, what your budget actually buys, and which neighborhoods NYC transplants choose most.

The commute: is it actually doable?

Yes — it's Stamford's whole appeal. The Stamford Transportation Center is the second-busiest station in the Metro-North system, and express trains reach Grand Central in about 50 minutes (locals run a bit longer). Peak-hour trains leave every few minutes, and the station also has Amtrak (including Acela) for trips beyond the city. Plenty of Stamford professionals commute in 2–3 days a week and work hybrid the rest — which makes the math even better.

What you get for your money

This is where the move pays off. In Manhattan, your housing budget buys square feet measured in the hundreds; in Stamford it buys actual rooms — or a waterfront condo with amenities for the price of a small NYC one-bedroom. As of mid-2026, Stamford's median home price runs in the $650,000–$750,000 range, while comparable square footage in much of Manhattan easily tops $1.5M (Redfin/Zillow, mid-2026; contact me for a live comp). Renters feel it immediately too, and many convert to buyers within a year or two. See current numbers on the Stamford market page.

Where NYC transplants tend to land

  • South End / Harbor Point — the most "NYC-like" option: new luxury apartment towers, a waterfront boardwalk, restaurants, shuttle to the train. Walkable, amenity-rich, transit-first.
  • Downtown — high-rise condos and rentals steps from Bedford Street's dining and nightlife.
  • Shippan & Cove — waterfront homes for those who want a yard and the Sound.
  • North Stamford & Westover — space, trees, and space and room to grow.

Taxes & the money stuff

Connecticut has a state income tax, but movers leaving NYC also drop the New York City local income tax, which changes the comparison meaningfully for high earners. Property taxes are based on Stamford's mill rate and your assessment. The right move is to run your specific numbers with a CPA — but for a lot of NYC households, the total picture improves. (Try the mortgage calculator to ballpark a monthly payment.)

What surprises people (in a good way)

Miles of Long Island Sound shoreline and beaches (Cove Island, Cummings), a genuinely walkable downtown, a big-city dining scene on Bedford Street, and major parks like Mill River — all in a city you can still leave for Manhattan in under an hour. Read more in Living in Stamford .

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a car when I move from NYC to Stamford?

For the Manhattan commute, no — the train is reliable and fast. For day-to-day life (groceries, weekend trips, suburban errands), most Stamford residents do keep a car. Downtown and Harbor Point are the most walkable areas where car-free living is genuinely feasible; push further into Shippan, North Stamford, or Westover and a car becomes nearly essential.

Will I save money moving from NYC to Stamford?

For most households, yes. Housing costs significantly less per square foot, and Connecticut residents don't pay New York City's local income tax (which tops out near 3.9% for higher earners). Connecticut does have its own state income tax, so run the exact numbers with a CPA for your income. See the full cost-of-living breakdown →

How long does it take to find a home in Stamford?

In Stamford's low-inventory market, desirable homes at fair prices move in days, not weeks. Most buyers I work with find their home within 4–10 weeks of getting fully pre-approved and starting their active search. The buyers who take longest are the ones who aren't pre-approved yet when they start looking — that slows everything down.

Which Stamford neighborhoods do most NYC transplants choose?

South End / Harbor Point is the top pick for those wanting the most NYC-like experience — walkable, transit-adjacent, new construction. Downtown suits condo buyers who want nightlife and dining steps away. Shippan attracts those wanting waterfront Single-Family homes. North Stamford suits buyers prioritizing space and privacy.
