Investment Property in Stamford, CT

Multi-family homes and rental condos — sourced, underwritten with real comps, and set up to rent. Numbers first, story second.

Short answer: I help investors buy 2–4 unit multi-family homes and rental condos in Stamford. Every candidate gets a cap-rate and cash-flow workup built from actual sale and rent comps — not listing-sheet estimates — before you offer. After closing, I help you price the rent, list the unit, and get it leased. No upfront fee; compensation is agreed in writing up front.

What's included

  • Sourcing: multi-family (2–4 unit) and condo candidates matched to your budget and strategy, from the live MLS feed plus off-market awareness — properties I hear about through agent-to-agent outreach before or without a public listing.
  • Cap-rate analysis: net operating income against price, built from real Stamford rent comps, taxes, insurance, and realistic vacancy and maintenance lines — not the pro-forma on the listing sheet.
  • Cash-flow modeling: what the property actually nets per month at your down payment and rate, including common charges on condos.
  • Comps from a private database: I maintain my own MLS comps database, refreshed every 3 hours, so rent and sale comparisons reflect the market this week — not a stale estimate.
  • Offer strategy and negotiation through inspection, appraisal, and closing.
  • Landlord setup: rent pricing, listing and marketing the unit, and a walkthrough of Connecticut's rental application and lease process.

How I run the numbers differently

Most investment listings are sold on a story. I underwrite them on data. My comps database pulls from the MLS every 3 hours, so when I tell you what a Glenbrook two-family's units should rent for, that's built from what comparable units are actually asking and leasing at right now. For context, Stamford currently has 1,104 properties listed for sale and 582 active rentals, with a median asking rent of $3,850/mo — and those numbers on this page refresh automatically.

The other difference is honesty about the math: most listings don't pencil, and I'll tell you which ones don't and why, before you spend money on inspections. A deal that only works with best-case rents and zero vacancy isn't a deal. You can pressure-test the sale side yourself on the live market page or browse inventory on the search.

Multi-family vs. rental condos in Stamford

Small multi-families (2–4 units) give you multiple rents from one purchase and one roof. Stamford's 2–4 unit housing stock is concentrated in neighborhoods like Glenbrook, Cove, Springdale, and the West Side, and these buildings vary a lot in condition and layout — which is exactly where a unit-by-unit rent workup earns its keep. They also work for house-hacking: live in one unit, rent the others.

Rental condos are the lower-entry path: typically less capital in, less maintenance on you, and plenty of renter demand near downtown and the train. The catch is common charges and any building rental rules — both go straight into the cash-flow model before you offer. Start with what's on the market at condos for sale in Stamford, and gut-check rents against current Stamford rentals.

Either way, carrying costs matter as much as rent: see Stamford's property tax rate for the tax line of the model.

Landlord setup, after the closing

Owning the unit is half the job — leasing it is the other half. After closing I help you set the rent from live comps, get the unit listed and marketed, and walk you through Connecticut's rental application and lease process, all of it Fair-Housing compliant. Prefer to be hands-off? I'll connect you with property management options and stay available on renewals and repricing as the comps move.

Weighing whether to keep renting or buy your own place instead? That's a different analysis — see rent vs. buy in Stamford. And if you're buying a home to live in rather than to rent out, my buyer's agent service covers that; if you already own and want to know what it's worth, start with a home valuation.

FAQ

What does it cost to work with you on an investment property?

There's no upfront fee. I work as your buyer's agent, and my compensation is agreed in writing before we tour anything — in many transactions some or all of it is paid through the deal itself. We go through exactly how it works, line by line, in our first conversation.

What kinds of investment properties are there in Stamford?

Two main paths: small multi-family buildings (2–4 units), concentrated in neighborhoods like Glenbrook, Cove, Springdale, and the West Side, and rental condos across the city at a lower entry price. Multi-families mean more doors per purchase; condos are simpler to run, but common charges have to be built into the math.

Do you help after closing, once I own the unit?

Yes. Landlord setup is part of the service: rent pricing from real comps, listing and marketing the unit, and a walkthrough of the Connecticut application and lease process. If you'd rather be hands-off, I can connect you with property management options.

Want the numbers on a specific property?

Send me an address or a budget and I'll run the cap-rate and cash-flow workup from real comps — and tell you straight if it doesn't pencil. More about how I work is on the about page.
