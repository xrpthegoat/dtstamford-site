Living in Downtown Stamford

Downtown Stamford is the city's most walkable district: a compact grid of high-rise condo towers and rental buildings wrapped around Bedford Street, Bank Street, and Columbus Park. Ground-floor restaurants, cafés, and nightlife mean most errands are a short walk, and Mill River Park adds green space and a greenway right at the edge of the core.

Downtown Stamford real estate & home prices

The housing stock is overwhelmingly condominiums and rental apartments, from renovated pre-war buildings to newer glass towers with amenities like doormen, gyms, and roof decks. Entry-level one-bedroom condos often start in the mid-$300Ks (estimated), with larger two-bedroom and penthouse units running well higher depending on the building and view. Getting around & commuting

The Stamford Transportation Center is walking distance, putting Metro-North express trains to Grand Central at roughly 50 minutes. I-95 and the Merritt Parkway are both minutes away, and the walkable layout makes a car optional for many downtown residents.

Parks, dining & landmarks

Landmarks include the Palace Theatre, Columbus Park, Mill River Park and its greenway, and the Bedford/Bank Street dining and nightlife corridor. For the wider picture, see the full Stamford neighborhoods guide or the 2026 Stamford market report.

Downtown Stamford: quick answers

How much are condos in Downtown Stamford?

Downtown is mostly condos and rentals; one-bedroom condos often start in the mid-$300Ks and rise from there by size, building, and view (estimated ranges — ask for current listings).

Is Downtown Stamford walkable?

Yes — it's the most walkable part of the city, with restaurants, parks, and the train station all within a short walk, so many residents live car-light.

How far is Downtown Stamford from NYC?

The Stamford Transportation Center is walkable from Downtown, with Metro-North express service to Grand Central in about 50 minutes.
