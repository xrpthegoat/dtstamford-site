Stamford Schools Appoint New Assistant Superintendent Amid Leadership Transition

Stamford schools appoint Scott Clayton as new assistant superintendent, ensuring stability in secondary education leadership.

Uploaded · July 21, 2026

The Stamford Board of Education has appointed Scott Clayton as the new assistant superintendent for secondary education, effective immediately following a unanimous vote on July 15, 2026 [2]. This appointment comes after the previous hire, Matthew Laskowski, accepted a principal role at Western Middle School in Greenwich just days after being named to the Stamford position [2]. Clayton, who previously served as director of student data and systems in the district, will now oversee middle schools, high schools, and adult and continuing education programs [1].

What's happening

Scott Clayton was formally approved by the Stamford Board of Education on July 15, 2026, after a unanimous vote [2]. The appointment is effective immediately, replacing Matthew Laskowski, who left the role less than a week after being named to it, opting instead for a principal position in Greenwich [2]. Clayton brings over 20 years of educational leadership experience, including 12 years as principal of Scofield Magnet Middle School, where the school earned multiple regional and state honors, including being named a Spotlight School by the New England League of Middle Schools in 2015, 2018, and 2023 [1]. He also served as assistant principal at Newtown High School and began his career as an eighth-grade U.S. history teacher at Rippowam Magnet Middle School [2]. Most recently, Clayton led the district’s data and technology integration teams as director of student data and systems [1]. He holds a bachelor’s degree in history from Western Connecticut State University, a master’s in secondary education from the University of Bridgeport, a sixth-year certificate in educational leadership from Sacred Heart University, and superintendent certification from Central Connecticut State University [2]. He is certified by the Connecticut State Department of Education in superintendent, intermediate administration, and grades 4–12 history and social studies [2].

Leadership stability and district continuity

The swift replacement of the assistant superintendent for secondary education signals a commitment to maintaining operational continuity within Stamford Public Schools. The fact that the Board of Education moved quickly to appoint Clayton—just days after Laskowski’s departure—suggests that leadership transitions are being managed with urgency and intentionality. Clayton’s deep familiarity with the district, having served in multiple leadership roles across schools and central offices, positions him well to maintain momentum on existing initiatives. His background in data systems and instructional leadership indicates a focus on evidence-based decision-making, which may influence how resources are allocated and how student performance is tracked across the district.
